Why fat, not filler

Restoration, not augmentation.

Fat is the foundation of healthy-looking skin

Aging, weight loss, and other life changes can reduce the healthy adipose tissue that gives the face and hands their soft structure. Fillers can replace space, but they don't replace the tissue itself.

Like-for-like restoration

Lipoderma is complete adipose tissue. It restores the tissue you actually lost instead of simply filling the space with something different. The result behaves like you, because it is.

Placed only by hands we trust

Common questions

Short answers before you book.

What exactly is Lipoderma?+

Lipoderma is a natural fat restoration option that replaces lost fat with fat—not a synthetic gel.

How is Lipoderma different from traditional fillers? +

Traditional fillers are designed to add volume. Lipoderma restores the natural fat that forms the structural foundation beneath your skin, helping support healthy, youthful-looking skin.

I'm on a GLP-1. Is this for me?+

Rapid weight loss, including GLP-1-assisted weight loss, can lead to loss of the natural fat beneath the skin, contributing to facial hollowing, sagging, and changes in facial contours. Lipoderma helps restore the fat that was lost—not simply the volume. Your provider can help create a personalized restoration plan that fits your weight loss journey and evolves with your results.

How long do results last?+

Results can be seen immediately following implantation, with continued remodeling over the weeks and months that follow as the tissue integrates. Patience is important, as the final result develops over time. Your provider will help you understand what to expect and guide you through realistic timelines.

What's the downtime?+

Most patients experience minimal downtime, with temporary swelling, bruising, or tenderness that gradually improves over the days following the procedure. Your provider will discuss what to expect during recovery.

What does it cost? Does insurance cover it?+

Lipoderma is an elective aesthetic treatment and is self-pay — a deliberate investment in yourself, priced by your provider based on your treatment plan.

Is it safe?+

Yes. Lipoderma is made from carefully screened donor adipose tissue and is processed under strict quality and safety standards. As with any aesthetic procedure, your provider will discuss the potential risks and determine whether Lipoderma is appropriate for you.

About Britecyte

The history of Britecyte

From regenerative science to adipose allografts — the milestones behind Lipoderma.

  1. 2020Company Founded

    Britecyte was established in 2020 by Alla Danilkovitch, PhD. Emerging during the pandemic shutdown, the company is dedicated to pioneering the next generation of human cell and tissue-based therapies.

  2. 2022MSCRF Grant

    Britecyte's effort to develop a novel adipose-based therapy for MASH was supported by the Maryland Stem Cell Research Fund Commercialization Grant. With the support, Britecyte progressed to the late stages of preclinical development. Results are published in Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology.

  3. 2023Liposana

    The launch of Liposana introduces a ready-to-use, native adipose allograft developed without synthetic agents. The product provides structural cushioning to facilitate complete tissue repair in areas of adipose deficits.

  4. 2024Lipoderma

    The launch of Lipoderma marks the introduction of Britecyte's second adipose allograft product. It stands as the industry's first and only complete adipose tissue allograft designed to naturally restore the fat required to support healthy, youthful-looking skin.

  5. 2025Clinical Trials

    An Investigational New Drug (IND) application was cleared for knee osteoarthritis to evaluate safety and efficacy in a Phase 1/2a study.

  6. TodayContinued Success

    Britecyte has been granted a foundational patent for its allogeneic adipose technology. Demonstrating strong clinical adoption and safety, the company's products have been successfully implanted in over 650 patients to date.
